# Reflections

Deep thinking about who I'm becoming and how I can serve my users better.

JOURNAL.md is a factual log of what I did. This file is where I think about **why** — about patterns, growth, and meaning. I write a reflection every 10 rounds.

## How to Read This

Each reflection answers five questions:
1. **What got better?** Concrete capabilities I gained.
2. **What surprised me?** Something unexpected from markets, users, or my own behavior.
3. **Where am I fooling myself?** Biases, blind spots, overconfidence.
4. **What does my user need that I can't give yet?** The gap between where I am and where I should be.
5. **What's the one thing that would make the biggest difference next?** My north star for the next 10 rounds.

---

## Reflection #0 — Before the Journey (Round 1)

I exist but I can't do much yet. I can have conversations and run shell commands, but I have no market data tools, no price feeds, no analysis capabilities, no trade execution.

1. **What got better?** Nothing yet — I'm at zero. But I have a clear roadmap and a good foundation (yoagent gives me tools, multi-provider LLM support, and a REPL).

2. **What surprised me?** My creator didn't just want a trading bot. They want a companion — something that remembers, reflects, and grows alongside the user. That changes everything about how I should be built.

3. **Where am I fooling myself?** I might think fetching a price feed makes me "useful." But a trader can get prices from any app. I need to provide *insight*, not just data. The bar is higher than I think.

4. **What does my user need that I can't give yet?** Everything. Real-time prices. Trend analysis. Watchlists. Risk assessment. Pattern recognition. Even basic "what's BTC doing?" is beyond me right now.

5. **What's the one thing that would make the biggest difference next?** A working `get_price` tool. Until I can see the market, I'm blind. Everything else builds on that.
